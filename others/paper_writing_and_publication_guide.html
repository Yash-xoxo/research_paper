Your complete paper writing guide

Every section — exactly what to write, how long, what to include

1
Title + Abstract ~250 words · Write last
Title
Use: "CNN-Based ABO Blood Group Classification from Microscopic Blood Images with Explainable AI" — contains: method (CNN), task (ABO classification), data (microscopic images), novelty (XAI)
Abstract
5 sentences exactly: (1) Why blood typing matters. (2) Gap — current methods are manual/slow/unexplainable. (3) What you did — CNN + MobileNetV2 + Grad-CAM. (4) Your result — "achieved X% accuracy on [dataset]." (5) Significance — faster, explainable, AI-assisted.
Write this last — only when you have real results. Never fabricate numbers.
2
Introduction ~500 words · Write in Week 1
Para 1
Hook: Blood typing is critical for transfusions, transplants, emergency care. Over 100 million blood transfusions happen yearly. Manual testing is slow, error-prone, requires reagents.
Para 2
AI motivation: Deep learning has transformed medical imaging — cancer, malaria, COVID detection. Blood cell analysis is a growing field. But ABO classification from images is underexplored.
Para 3
Gap statement: "Existing CNN-based blood group work (IEEE 2022) achieved 96.7% but compared only against outdated architectures (AlexNet, LeNet5) and provided no explainability." — THIS is your gap.
Para 4
Your contribution: List 3 bullet points — (1) modern lightweight CNN, (2) comparison with MobileNetV2/EfficientNet, (3) Grad-CAM explainability for clinical trust.
Para 5
Paper structure: "The rest of this paper is organized as follows: Section II reviews…" — one sentence each section.
3
Literature Review / Related Work ~600 words · Write in Week 1
Group A
Blood cell classification (3–4 papers): Acevedo et al. 2020 (PBC dataset), Gavas & Olpadkar 2021 (27 CNNs, 99.51%), Islam et al. 2024 (XAI + 99.12%). Show state of the art.
Group B
Blood GROUP detection (3–4 papers): IEEE 2022 ABO paper (96.7%), IJCTT 2025 (K-means + SVM), IJSCI 2025 (agglutination images). Show what's been tried for ABO specifically.
Group C
Explainable AI in medical imaging (2 papers): Islam et al. 2024 Grad-CAM++, CMES 2025 lightweight XAI. Then say: "However, none of these XAI studies targeted ABO blood group classification."
End para
Conclude the gap: "To the best of our knowledge, no prior work has combined lightweight CNN architectures with XAI techniques specifically for ABO blood group classification, which this paper addresses."
4
Dataset Description ~300 words · Write in Week 1–2
Content
Name + source of dataset used. Total images. Class distribution (A, B, AB, O — count per class). Image resolution. Train/validation/test split ratios (70/15/15). Where you downloaded it from.
Table
Add a small table: Class | No. of images | % of total. This shows you understand your data distribution.
If custom
If you build your own dataset: describe collection process, microscope settings, how labels were assigned, ethical approval if applicable. This becomes an extra research contribution.
If using Barcelona PBC dataset, note it doesn't have ABO labels but is used to evaluate morphology classification pipeline, and frame accordingly.
5
Proposed Methodology ~700 words · Write in Week 2–3 (the core section)
5A
Preprocessing: Resize to 224×224. CLAHE contrast enhancement. Gaussian blur (kernel 3×3). Normalization [0,1]. Justify each step — don't just list, explain why.
5B
Augmentation: Rotation (±20°), horizontal flip, zoom (0.9–1.1×), brightness shift (±15%). State you used ImageDataGenerator (Keras) and the augmentation was applied only on training set.
5C
CNN architecture: Describe layer by layer — Conv2D → MaxPool → BatchNorm → Dropout → Dense → Softmax. Include a table with layer names, filter sizes, output shapes. Draw a diagram.
5D
Transfer learning: Describe MobileNetV2 base (frozen ImageNet weights), added Dense layers, fine-tuning strategy (unfreeze last N layers). Compare with your custom CNN.
5E
Training setup: Optimizer (Adam, lr=0.001), loss (categorical crossentropy), batch size (32), epochs (50), early stopping (patience=5), ModelCheckpoint (save best val_acc).
5F
Grad-CAM: Explain what it is (1 sentence), which layer you apply it to (last conv layer), show 2–3 example heatmap images. This is your novelty — describe it clearly.
6
Experimental Results ~500 words · Write in Week 3
Metrics
Report for each model: Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-score, AUC-ROC — per class AND overall. Use a results table. This is mandatory for publication.
Figures
(1) Training/validation accuracy curve. (2) Training/validation loss curve. (3) Confusion matrix (4×4 for A/B/AB/O). (4) Grad-CAM heatmap examples. These 4 figures are required.
Comparison
Table comparing: Your CNN vs MobileNetV2 vs AlexNet vs LeNet5 (from IEEE 2022). Show you beat the 96.7% benchmark — or honestly explain why you didn't and what you would improve.
7
Discussion ~400 words · Write in Week 4
Content
Interpret your results — not just state them. Why did MobileNetV2 perform better/worse than custom CNN? What do the Grad-CAM maps reveal? Which class (A/B/AB/O) was hardest to classify and why? What does that tell us about the biology?
Limitations
Be honest: small dataset, no Rh factor, single population data, not clinically validated. Showing you know the limits is a sign of good research, not weakness.
8
Conclusion + Future Work ~250 words · Write in Week 4
Content
Summarize what you did and what accuracy you got. Then 3 future work points: (1) extend to Rh+ /Rh− (8 classes), (2) build larger labeled dataset, (3) deploy as mobile/edge application for resource-limited settings.

Where to publish — by difficulty

★ Easiest — Start here
arXiv preprint
Free. Upload PDF. Live in 24–48hrs. Gets you a DOI and citation-ready link. Submit to cs.CV or eess.IV category. Not peer-reviewed but builds credibility immediately.

🔗 arxiv.org/submit
★★ Fast journal (2–6 weeks)
MDPI Applied Sciences
Scopus + Web of Science indexed. Open access. Fast peer review (2–6 weeks). APC fee ~CHF 2400 — check for waiver. Has published many similar CNN medical imaging papers.

🔗 mdpi.com/journal/applsci
★★ Fast journal (4–6 weeks)
IEEE Access
IEEE-branded, fully open access, Scopus indexed. APC ~$1,995 USD. Accepts applied deep learning medical imaging papers. Peer review 4–6 weeks average.

🔗 ieeeaccess.ieee.org
★★★ Conference (stronger)
IEEE ICIP / EMBC
ICIP (Image Processing) or EMBC (Engineering in Medicine & Biology). 4–6 page paper. Peer reviewed. Published on IEEE Xplore. Check 2026 deadlines — typically Jan–March submission.

🔗 ieeexplore.ieee.org
★★★ Scopus fast AI journal
Frontiers in AI / Bioeng.
Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence or Frontiers in Bioengineering & Biotechnology. Open access, Scopus indexed. Review 4–8 weeks. APC ~€1,390–€2,150. Good fit for XAI medical papers.
★★★★ Prestigious (slower)
Computers in Biology & Medicine
Elsevier, high-impact. IF ~7. Requires strong dataset + solid methodology. Review 3–6 months. Free to submit (non-OA). Best if you have a novel dataset. Target after arXiv/conference.
Recommended path for 1 month: Finish paper → upload to arXiv (Day 28) → simultaneously submit to MDPI Applied Sciences or IEEE Access. You get a public preprint immediately while peer review runs in parallel (takes 4–6 weeks). Best of both worlds.
