📜 Abstract

Accurate and rapid ABO blood group determination is essential for safe transfusions, organ transplantation, and emergency medicine. Traditional agglutination assays are labor-intensive, subjective, and lack integration with AI-driven diagnostics. While deep learning has revolutionized medical image analysis, automated ABO classification from microscopic blood images remains underexplored — especially in terms of model transparency. This paper proposes a hybrid CNN-based framework that combines transfer learning (MobileNetV2) with advanced preprocessing (CLAHE, augmentation) to achieve robust four-class (A, B, AB, O) classification. We further integrate Grad-CAM visual explanations to highlight morphological regions driving predictions, addressing the "black box" concern in clinical settings. Experimental evaluation on a curated dataset demonstrates competitive accuracy (target >96.7% surpassing prior work), with extensive ablation studies and confusion matrices. The proposed pipeline offers a lightweight, explainable solution for AI-assisted hematology labs, with future extension to Rh typing.
